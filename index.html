About
```

I hold a B.S. in Data Science and Mathematics from Tecnológico de Monterrey (ITESM). I currently collaborate with The Carter Center, where I build LLM-based workflows to analyze and structure legal texts on freedom of expression and association.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked across both industry and research. At CEMEX, I built SQL-based data systems and dashboards for operational metrics. At Concordia University, I worked on automating data extraction for the GRNDS project, compiling global NGO regulation data.

I’m interested in applied LLM systems, especially retrieval-augmented generation, agents, and evaluation. Long term, I want to build AI systems that are accessible, fair, and useful in high-impact domains such as policy and climate, with a focus on ensuring Latin America is not left behind.
```
