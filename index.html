about

I hold a B.S. in Data Science and Mathematics from Tecnológico de Monterrey (ITESM). My work sits at the intersection of applied AI, policy, and real-world deployment.

I currently collaborate with The Carter Center, where I build LLM-based workflows to analyze legal texts on freedom of expression and association. The goal is to help create structured, cross-country datasets that support policy research and legal analysis.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked across both research and industry. At CEMEX, I built SQL-based data systems and dashboards for operational metrics. At Concordia University, I worked on AI pipelines for extracting and structuring legal and NGO-regulation data.

paper / legal ai
