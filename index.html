About

I currently collaborate with The Carter Center. I've built LLM-based workflows to codify laws on freedom of expression. This work contributes to building structured, cross-country datasets for policy research.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked across both industry and research. At CEMEX, I built SQL-based data systems and dashboards for operational metrics. At Concordia University, I worked on automating data extraction for the GRNDS project, an initiative that compiles and standardizes data from national charity registers worldwide.

I’m interested in applied LLM research, especially agent-based RAG systems and evaluation. Long term, I want to help build AI systems that are accessible, fair, and sustainable, and that support people and institutions in high-impact domains such as policy and climate. I care especially about ensuring Mexico and Latin America as a whole have a role in the development and use of these systems.

LLMs for Comparative Legal Analysis of NGO Regulations

Developed a workflow using large language models to analyze and classify NGO-related laws across multiple countries. The project explored how prompting strategies, model selection, and multilingual legal texts affect accuracy, consistency, and instruction-following in legal coding tasks.
